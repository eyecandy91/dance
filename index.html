About Mulvihill irish Dance Academy

The Academy’s goal is to teach every dancer to the best of their ability and to provide numerous opportunities for learning and success. Classes are available to boys and girls from age 4+ and in dance level from beginner through championship. Students have the opportunity to perform and compete on a local, national and international level.

At the Mulvihill Academy, dancers will learn to Irish dance in a fun, friendly and positive environment. Irish dance is a healthy activity that not only provides great exercise, but also helps develop confidence. It improves concentration, increases balance and coordination and promotes structure and discipline.

All classes are taught by Rachel Mulvihill, a qualified TCRG registered with Cumann Rince Dea Mheasa. Relevant police checks (DBS), insurance, first aid and child protection policies are all in place.

Meet the Mulvihill teachers

Born and raised in South East London, Rachel began Irish Dancing at the age of 9. She danced for The Fryday School of Irish Dancing before moving onto one of the most successful Irish Dance schools in the world taught by Kathleen Maguire A.D.C.R.G and Michael Maguire A.D.C.R.G.

Debbie was also born and raised in South East London and began dancing at the age of 11 after being inspired after seeing Riverdance. She danced with the Frydays Academy where she performed and took part in many events across the country and internationally being part of the winning team at the Great British Championships twice as well as the World Dance Championships in Barcelona.

our aims & goals

We aim to encourage young aspiring artists not only to dance and perform but to be confident, sociable and team players. With this attitude, children automatically make new friends. This is achieved through developing a sense of community and opens up children’s imaginations and confidence in their creativity; discovering channels of communication and ideas previously untapped.
